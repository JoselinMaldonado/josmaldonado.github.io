About me

Full-time PhD student in Optical Science at the Optics Research Center. I am a biomedical engineer with a Master's degree in Applied Sciences. Currently, I am working in the area of digital interferometry, applying phase-shifting algorithms that allow obtaining the geometric profile of a surface, faster and more efficiently.

Also, I had worked in the development of electronic circuits and signal processing for the area of electrical bioimpedance. The goal was to enhance the instrumentation of the area by providing a device with a bandwidth exceeding 100 kHz and the capability to inject various injection current values to obtain distinct impedance measurements.

I am a responsible, organized, committed, constant, perseverant, creative, and a fast learner. English is my second language, which I have reinforced through reading and writing scientific articles, as well as volunteering abroad. My academic goal is to join international scientific collaboration networks, developing optical solutions for biomedical applications.

Latest Research

  • Development of a high-resolution blind-phase retrieval technique using color fringe patterns. The main goal was to separate the color channels and to correct the detuning induced.
  • Development of a technique to reduce the errors of geometrical distortion in the retrieved profile due to misalignment of the projectors and high slopes.
